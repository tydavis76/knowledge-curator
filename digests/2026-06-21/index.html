    Source: ACM Queue | Domain: Distributed Systems | ~14 min

    Why AI hasn't replaced software engineers, and won't¶

    Source: Simon Willison's Weblog | Domain: Ai Systems | ~10 min

    Why it matters: Narayanan and Kappor's causal framework for why task automation ≠ job displacement — the distinction between automating subtasks vs. automating the judgment layer that coordinates them is a durable lens for any AI-labor substitution argument.

    Jagged Intelligence: The Dangerous Unknowns at the Heart of LLMs¶

    Source: Complexity Digest | Domain: Ai Systems | ~14 min

    Why it matters: Melanie Mitchell's 'jagged intelligence' frame gives you a precise mental model for reasoning about where LLM capability boundaries actually lie — not smooth degradation but cliff-edge failures adjacent to competence — which directly changes how you design systems that depend on them.

    AI-Native Firms¶

    Source: Marginal Revolution | Domain: Economics Finance | ~12 min

    Why it matters: Empirical HBS/INSEAD research on how firms built around AI capabilities organize differently from traditional startups — changes how you reason about team structure, hiring leverage, and where human bottlenecks persist in AI-first orgs.

    Why the Human Genome's Tangled Physicality May Confound AI¶

    Source: Quanta Magazine | Domain: Science Complexity | ~14 min

    Why it matters: The argument that the genome is not an algorithm but a physical substrate with entangled causal structure is a precise, generalizable critique of when symbolic/statistical AI approaches hit fundamental limits — applicable well beyond biology.

    Heterogeneity for Flocking and Computation: From Biology to Mathematics¶

    Source: Complexity Digest | Domain: Science Complexity | ~10 min

    Why it matters: Formalizes why heterogeneity in distributed agents (not uniformity) drives robust collective computation — a durable model with direct implications for designing fault-tolerant distributed systems and multi-agent AI architectures.

    Behind the Scenes: Block 450 JVM Repositories Into Monorepo to Reduce Dependency Drift¶

    Source: InfoQ | Domain: Distributed Systems | ~12 min

    Why it matters: Migrating 450 repos at this scale surfaces the real coordination-cost topology of a large engineering org — the dependency drift framing is a durable lens for evaluating polyrepo vs. monorepo tradeoffs beyond the usual DX arguments.

    Inside Atlassian's Forge Billing Architecture for Distributed Usage Tracking at Scale¶

    Source: InfoQ | Domain: Distributed Systems | ~12 min

    Why it matters: Usage-based billing at cloud scale is an underexplored distributed-systems problem; this exposes the idempotency, deduplication, and layered-storage design decisions that recur in any metering-critical pipeline.

    Presentation: Write-Ahead Intent Log: A Foundation for Efficient CDC at Scale¶

    Source: InfoQ | Domain: Distributed Systems | ~18 min

    Why it matters: Building a custom CDC layer above heterogeneous stores by treating the intent log as a first-class primitive reframes how you reason about change propagation — the 'dumb producer, smart consumer' pattern has wide applicability beyond this use case.

    Presentation: AI Agents to Make Sense of Data at OpenAI¶

    Why it matters: Kepler's architecture — scoped semantic memory, MCP for context-window management, and RAG over 600+ PB — is one of the few public post-mortems on what actually breaks when you run LLM agents against real production data infrastructure.
